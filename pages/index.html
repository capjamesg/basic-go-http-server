Hello! I'm James (also capjamesg). My pronouns are he/him/his. I live in Scotland, UK. This is my blog.

I like to build for the web. I am experimenting with functional programming and want to learn more about computer science.

On this blog, I share my thoughts on all things coffee and I interview people who work in coffee. I sometimes write about websites and web development. I also play the Melodica.

My email address is readers@jamesg.blog. I sometimes post on Instagram.

I also contribute to the IndieWeb wiki. I have open sourced some code on GitHub and Sourcehut.

The next event I am going to is Beyond Tellerand Berlin 2022.

  • Separating I/O and logic

    Published on August 05, 2022 under the IndieWeb category.

    Earlier this week I watched Alex Chan's Sans I/O programming talk. In the talk, Alex argues the importance of separating I/O and program logic, with reference to a situation where his team was unable to use already-available parsing libraries for BagIt data because said libraries depended on local access to a file. This talk resonated with me because it made me realise I tend to couple I/O and logic in my code without realising it. I'd highly recommend watching the talk in full, or at least reading Alex's accompanying summary on their blog.
